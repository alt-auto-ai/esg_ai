The Australian Energy Market Commission's recommendation to continue the wholesale demand response mechanism has implications for energy efficiency in the national electricity market, says the AEMC.

The AEMC highlights that the WDRM allows large electricity users to reduce consumption during peak demand, benefiting all electricity consumers.

It anticipates initiating a rule change in the first half of 2026 to assess participation from sites with multiple connection points.

"Seeing signs consistent with H5 bird flu in wildlife on Heard Island is not unexpected," says the Joint DAFF & DCCEEW media statement.

"The Australian Government is investing more than $100 million into strengthening bird flu preparedness and enhancing our response capacity," the statement says.

"A confirmed detection on Heard Island would not substantially increase the risk to Australia.", the statemend adds.

Statutory development – Biosecurity appointment.-The Labor Government has appointed Dr Melissa McEwen as the new Inspector-General of Biosecurity.

The government has appointed Dr McEwen to enhance biosecurity systems across Australia, unless otherwise specified by regulatory exceptions.

The government has also invested over $2 billion since 2022 to strengthen biosecurity, aiming to mitigate risks from exotic pests and diseases by the end of 2025.

The Northern Territory and Queensland have joined New South Wales as members of the PFAS National Coordinating Body, strengthening national collaboration on the management of per- and poly-fluoroalkyl substances (PFAS).

“Communities affected by PFAS contamination need decisive action. That’s why we’re expanding the PFAS National Coordinating Body to ensure support reaches those who need it most, right across Australia,” Assistant Minister for Defence, Peter Khalil, said.

Australia and the United States established a bilateral framework to secure supply chains for critical minerals and rare earths, essential for defense and advanced technologies.

This initiative includes a commitment of at least $1 billion each from both countries towards an $8.5 billion pipeline of priority projects, highlighting significant investment in sustainable resource management.

The formation of a Critical Minerals Supply Security Response Group aims to address supply vulnerabilities, indicating a proactive approach to regulatory compliance and stakeholder engagement in the critical minerals sector, which is increasingly vital for economic resilience and environmental governance.

Meanwhile China has announced new export controls on critical minerals to manage supply chain vulnerabilities.

The Ministry of Commerce has imposed licensing requirements for exports of products containing Chinese-sourced rare earth materials, effective immediately.

The government reports that China accounted for 91% of global rare earth refining in 2024, with new controls expected to impact global supply chains significantly by 8 November.
